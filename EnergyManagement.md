# 1. Understanding Your Energy

### Q1: What helps you relax (Calm quadrant)?

- Sitting quietly
- Going for a slow walk
- Listening to calm music
- Light stretching
- Writing in a journal
- Just resting and doing nothing

### Q2: When do you feel stressed (Stress quadrant)?

- When you have too much work
- When something feels new or difficult
- When you don’t have enough time
- When you haven’t slept well
- When you try to do many things at once

### Q3: How do you know you’re excited (Excitement quadrant)?

- You feel happy and energetic
- You look forward to doing the task
- Time passes quickly
- You feel curious and interested
- Your body feels active but not tense

## 4. Sleep Is Your Superpower

### Q4: Paraphrase the Sleep is your Superpower video in your own words in brief. Only the points, no explanation.

- Sleep helps your brain remember things you learned. 
- Sleep helps your body stay healthy and strong (immune system). 
- Not sleeping enough increases your risk of heart attacks and other health problems. 
- Lack of sleep disrupts your body’s genes and health systems. 
- Better sleep comes from going to bed and waking up at the same time. 
- Sleeping in a cool room helps you sleep better.

### Q5: What are some ideas that you can implement to sleep better?

- Go to bed and wake up same time every day. 
- Keep your bedroom cool and comfortable. 
- Avoid heavy food, caffeine, or screens before bed.
- Do slow breathing or light stretching to relax.
- Reduce light or noise in your room.

## 5. Brain-Changing Benefits of Exercise

### Q6: Paraphrase the video - Brain Changing Benefits of Exercise. Minimum 5 points, only the points.

- Exercise boosts mood — you feel happier. 
- Exercise improves memory and learning ability. 
- Exercise sharpens focus and attention for a few hours. 
- Exercise helps grow and protect brain cells over time. 
- Exercise lowers risk of brain aging and diseases like Alzheimer’s. 
- Even one workout can help your brain chemistry immediately. 

### Q7: What are some steps you can take to exercise more?

- Start with short sessions — even 10 minutes counts.
- Pick activities you enjoy — dancing, walking, cycling.
- Do exercise at the same time every day to build a habit.
- Use reminders or alarms on your phone.
- Walk more — take stairs, park farther away, walk after meals.
- Exercise with a friend to stay motivated.
